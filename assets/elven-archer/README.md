# Elven Archer — Character Concept Art

Generated character concept inspired by a customizable elven female miniature.

Concept: an elegant elven archer with flowing hair and a leaf circlet, drawing an ornate elven longbow, with a slender rapier sheathed on her left hip. Standing on an elven forest base with mossy roots and small mushrooms.

## Print notes (v2)

Design changes made specifically to be more forgiving on a resin printer at ~25 micron layer heights:

- **No floating hair** — long strands are consolidated into fewer thick flow-locks that merge into the cape/shoulder. Eliminates island supports on the visible front.
- **Anchored cape** — hem thickened (~1mm scale) and one corner of the cape sculpted down onto the base, acting as a natural structural column and giving the whole cape backing support.
- **Bow held close and vertical** — bowstring thickened so it can actually print, arrow rests against the bow riser instead of floating in mid-air. In practice you'd still likely print the bow as a separate piece and glue it on, and thread real elastic for the string if you want scale-accurate thickness.
- **Chunkier leaf skirt** — fewer, thicker overlapping leaf plates instead of paper-thin ones.
- **Wider grounded stance** — larger footprint on the base = more plate stability and easier orientation.
- **Base**: thick mushroom stalks and fern fronds; roots merge with her boot and the cape corner for anchoring.

Recommended slicer setup (starting point, tune to your printer/resin):

- **Layer height:** 0.025mm (25µm) for the figure surface detail; you can go to 0.03–0.05mm on the base without a visible quality loss.
- **Orientation:** tilt ~30–45° with the back facing the plate so support scars land on the cloak and back, not the face.
- **Supports:** medium-density on the back of the cape, under the bow limb, and under any overhanging leaf plate; light supports on the mushrooms.
- **Wall thickness sanity:** nothing thinner than ~0.6mm at printed scale — if a detail comes out looking that thin in the sculpt at 32mm scale, thicken it.
